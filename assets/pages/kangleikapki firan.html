The Flag of Kangleipak
(A Symbol of Heritage and Identity)

The Flag of Kangleipak, also known as the Salai Taret Flag, is a rectangular seven-colored flag that holds deep historical and cultural significance for the Meitei ethnicity of Ancient Manipur. This flag is not merely a piece of cloth but a sacred symbol representing the seven clan dynasties that form the foundation of the Meitei community. Each color on the flag represents one of these clans, symbolizing their unique identities and contributions to the rich tapestry of Kangleipak’s history.

Mangang

"The Significance of Mangang and the Red Color in the Flag of Manipur"

Mangang, one of the seven illustrious clans of the Meetei, holds a special place among the Yek or Salai people of Manipur. The Mangang clan is a collection of various Yumnaks, the indigenous families of ancient Kangleipak, now known as Manipur, a vibrant state in India.

The red color in the flag of Manipur is a tribute to the Mangang clan. This vibrant hue symbolizes the time of Mangang's birth, coinciding with the rising sun, painting the sky in shades of red. The flag's colors draw inspiration from the sun's changing hues throughout the day, each representing the birth times of different clans' progenitors. This symbolism not only honors the Mangang clan but also celebrates the rich cultural heritage and traditions of Manipur.

Historical Significance and Symbolism

Colors and Clans: The Essence of Unity

The Flag of Kangleipak is adorned with seven distinct colors, each representing a specific clan dynasty:

  1. Red: Represents the Mangang clan.
  2. White: Represents the Luwang clan.
  3. Black: Represents the Khuman clan.
  4. Yellow: Represents the Angom clan.
  5. Red mixed with black: Represents the Moirang clan.
  6. Purple: Represents the Kha-Nganba clan.
  7. Light Sky blue: Represents the Salai-Leisangthem clan.

The width-to-length ratio of the flag is 2:3, a dimension approved by the Lainingthou Sanamahi Temple Board. This approval underscores the flag's sacred status within the community, as the Temple Board is a revered institution preserving the religious and cultural heritage of the Meitei people.

The Birth of Ibudhou Pakhangba's Seven Sons

The symbolism of the Flag of Kangleipak goes deeper, as it is intricately linked to the legend of Ibudhou Pakhangba and his seven sons. According to ancient Puya (sacred manuscripts), each of these sons represents one of the seven clans. The colors of the flag are inspired by the hues of the sun at different times of the day, corresponding to the birth times of these sons. This connection to celestial phenomena emphasizes the divine nature of the clans and their origins.

Cultural Practices and Dyeing Traditions

Dyeing Histories: More Than Just Colors

The colors of the Flag of Kangleipak are not arbitrary; they are the result of ancient dyeing traditions that have been meticulously preserved over centuries. The Amaibas and Amaibis (traditional priests and priestesses) continue to dye fabrics using these seven hues for Apok Khuramba, a sacred ritual involving the offering of dyed cloth. This practice highlights the deep spiritual connection the Meitei people have with the flag, viewing it as more than just a symbol, but a sacred artifact intertwined with their religious and cultural identity.

The Art of Dyeing: Techniques Passed Down Through Generations

The dyeing techniques used to create the colors of the flag are an integral part of Meitei heritage. These techniques involve the use of natural dyes extracted from plants, minerals, and other organic sources. The process is painstaking and requires a deep understanding of traditional knowledge passed down through generations. This meticulous care ensures that the colors remain vibrant and true to their original significance, preserving the cultural integrity of the flag.

The Flag as a Symbol of Heritage and Unity

A Testament to Rich Heritage

The Flag of Kangleipak serves as a testament to the rich heritage and lineage of the Meitei people. It honors the diverse identities within the community, celebrating the unique contributions of each clan. The flag is a unifying symbol, reminding the Meitei people of their shared history and collective identity.

Honoring Diverse Identities

Each clan represented on the flag has its own unique history, traditions, and contributions to the cultural mosaic of Kangleipak. The flag’s seven colors acknowledge and honor these differences, fostering a sense of pride and belonging among the Meitei people. This recognition of diversity within unity is a cornerstone of the Meitei identity, emphasizing the importance of each clan in the overall narrative of the community.

Contemporary Relevance and Preservation Efforts

Preserving the Flag’s Legacy

In modern times, efforts to preserve and promote the significance of the Flag of Kangleipak continue to be a priority for the Meitei community. The Lainingthou Sanamahi Temple Board plays a crucial role in these efforts, ensuring that the sacred status of the flag is maintained and respected. Educational programs and cultural initiatives aim to raise awareness about the flag’s history and symbolism, particularly among younger generations.

Cultural Revitalization and Education

Revitalizing traditional practices associated with the flag is an ongoing endeavor. Workshops, festivals, and community events are organized to celebrate and educate people about the flag’s importance. These initiatives help to keep the traditions alive, fostering a sense of continuity and connection to the past. By engaging in these cultural activities, the Meitei people reaffirm their commitment to preserving their heritage and passing it on to future generations.

Conclusion: The Enduring Legacy of the Flag of Kangleipak

The Flag of Kangleipak is more than just a flag; it is a profound symbol of heritage, unity, and identity for the Meitei people. Its colors and dimensions, rooted in ancient traditions and sacred histories, encapsulate the essence of the seven clan dynasties. As a testament to the rich cultural tapestry of Kangleipak, the flag continues to inspire and unite the Meitei community, serving as a beacon of their enduring legacy.
